Investigation Overview¶

With 168,019 confirmed cases and 6,610 deaths, 148 countries were affected (by March 16th) by the Coronavirus disease. This outbreak has gone way beyond what we expected. This pandemic caused WHO (World Health Organization) to raise the health emergency to its highest level along with many more countries' top emergency warnings. This project is targeted to briefly analyze the COVID-19 outbreak in the United States with some comparison against China (worst hit area) along with a rough prediction of the spreading trend.

Dataset Overview¶

Data set is operated by the Johns Hopkins University Center for Systems Science and Engineering (JHU CSSE). Also, Supported by ESRI Living Atlas Team and the Johns Hopkins University Applied Physics Lab (JHU APL). I extracted 2900 records cover information for two countries, 1682 records in China and 1218 records in the U.S.. Information is recorded from 2020 Jan 22nd until 2020 Mar 15th.

Confirmed cases geographic distribution¶

The plot shows the virus on record is between (-160, -65) in longitude and (20,65) in latitude.

More specifically, there are two humps in the Longitude distribution plot, they reflect more cases on both coasts. Other the other hand, there is a significant high volume in the latitude plot which occurs around 40 to 45 latitude. This points to WA, Northern CA, OR and Northern east coast states around NY.

Prediction¶

Following is a prediction model using logistic growth algorithm. In the title, max infections represent predicted maximum total confirmed cases within 30 days; r is the per capita rate of increase. The greater r is, the faster the rise to the peak. As the model shows, max confirmed cases within 30 days would likely be around 13,716, which will happen around second week in April.
